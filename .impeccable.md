# Impeccable Design Guidelines — Rial Archive

## Design Context

### Users
Iranians living abroad who check in on the Rial's performance for personal finance decisions — sending remittances home, converting savings, or simply staying connected to economic conditions back home. They visit with a quiet sense of urgency; the data often carries emotional weight. The interface should feel calm and reliable, never alarming or clinical. They are not necessarily traders — just people who need to trust the numbers they're seeing.

### Brand Personality
**Three words: Clear. Grounded. Honest.**

The app doesn't need to shout. It has a single, important job: show what the Rial is worth, accurately, over time. The voice is matter-of-fact, the visual language is restrained, and the design earns trust through precision and consistency — not decoration.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Style**: Clean and minimal — data-forward, generous whitespace, typography-led. Reference: Linear, Vercel, Resend.
- **Anti-references**: Avoid the over-decorated financial dashboard feel (too many borders, badges, gradients). Avoid the consumer-crypto flashiness (neon, glow effects, dark glass morphism for its own sake). Not Bloomberg-dense.
- **Theme**: Both light and dark modes are first-class citizens. Light feels like a clean paper ledger; dark feels like a late-night analysis session — both calm and intentional.
- **Color accent**: Green, derived from the existing logo (`#036704`). Use it sparingly and meaningfully — on key interactive elements, positive value indicators, and brand touchpoints. The UI chrome should be neutral; green appears when it matters.

### Color Palette

**Green accent scale (from logo `#036704`):**
- `--color-brand-50: #f0fdf4`
- `--color-brand-100: #dcfce7`
- `--color-brand-500: #22c55e`
- `--color-brand-600: #16a34a`
- `--color-brand-700: #15803d`
- `--color-brand-800: #166534`
- `--color-brand-900: #14532d`

**Use brand-600 (`#16a34a`) as the primary interactive color in light mode; brand-500 (`#22c55e`) in dark mode.**

**Neutrals (Zinc scale — warmer than gray, less clinical):**
- Light bg: `zinc-50` / `zinc-100`
- Light surface: `white`
- Light border: `zinc-200`
- Light text primary: `zinc-900`
- Light text secondary: `zinc-500`
- Dark bg: `zinc-950`
- Dark surface: `zinc-900`
- Dark border: `zinc-800`
- Dark text primary: `zinc-50`
- Dark text secondary: `zinc-400`

**Semantic colors (use sparingly, only for data meaning):**
- Positive/up: brand green
- Negative/down: `rose-500` (light) / `rose-400` (dark)
- Neutral change: `zinc-400`

### Typography
- **Font**: Keep Inter. It's excellent for financial data.
- **Hierarchy**:
  - Page title: `text-2xl font-semibold tracking-tight` — not oversized
  - Section label: `text-xs font-medium uppercase tracking-wider text-zinc-500` — quiet, structural
  - Data values: `text-sm font-mono` for numbers (use a monospace stack or `font-variant-numeric: tabular-nums`)
  - Body/labels: `text-sm font-normal`
- **Rule**: No decorative type. Every font decision serves readability.

### Spacing & Layout
- Prefer `zinc` border/bg over `gray` — slightly warmer, less corporate
- Card radius: `rounded-xl` (12px) — softer than current `rounded-lg`
- Use a single consistent gutter system: `gap-4` (16px) between controls, `gap-6` (24px) between sections
- The chart is the hero — give it full breathing room, minimal surrounding chrome
- Controls (date, currency, ROI) should feel like a toolbar, not a form
- Avoid box-in-box nesting: one background level for the page, one for cards — not deeper

### Component Voice
- **Buttons**: Ghost/outline style for secondary actions; filled green only for primary CTAs. Small, precise.
- **Dropdowns**: Clean, minimal — slim borders, no heavy shadows
- **Chart**: Lines over areas (area fill only as subtle wash at 10-15% opacity). No chart grid clutter.
- **Icons**: Keep Lucide. Use `w-4 h-4` consistently. Never decorative — icons clarify, they don't decorate.
- **Empty/loading states**: Simple skeleton or muted spinner. No full-page spinners.

### Design Principles

1. **Data is the interface.** The chart deserves 80% of the attention. Controls should feel like a thin, receded toolbar — present but never competing.

2. **Green earns its appearance.** The brand color should appear on interactive states, positive indicators, and brand elements only. The rest of the UI is zinc and white — the green pops because it's rare.

3. **Both themes are equal.** Every design decision must work in light and dark. Don't design in one and adapt the other. If it looks weak in dark mode, it's not done.

4. **Numbers deserve respect.** Financial data should be set in tabular/monospace numerals, aligned, precise. Currency values should never wrap, truncate unexpectedly, or feel approximate.

5. **Calm over cleverness.** This app is checked during moments of economic anxiety. Animations should be subtle (150–200ms ease). Transitions should reassure, not entertain. Nothing should surprise the user.
